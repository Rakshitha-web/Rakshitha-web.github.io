Welcome, my name is Jarrett Pratt, and my life's mission has been to support young minds in discovering their full potential in academia and in the classroom of life. As a licensed intervention specialist and Certified Inspired Leader by Cleveland State University I have gained invaluable professional experiences working in both public education systems, communities and higher education.

These opportunities have enabled me to blend theory with practice with a focus on Social Emotional Learning. I have a special interest in supporting diverse organizations and populations of learners, assisting them in unpacking the concept of “differing abilities” in organizational settings and exploring social-emotional learning theory in the context of diversity management and career mobility.

Jarrett Pratt is a resident of the Cleveland Heights, Ohio but a graduate of Piney Woods Country Life School in Piney Woods, Mississippi. Having Graduated from Fisk University in Nashville, Tennessee with a B.A in Political Science. Life’s experiences would lead him to the classroom where after several years teaching he earned a master’s degree in Education from Notre Dame College in South Euclid, Ohio.

Poetry & Spoken Word


J. Pratt’s poetic style is designed to stimulate interactions that appeal to multiple generations. His projects use engaging humor and cultural imagery to deliver inspirational content in the form of spoken word and poetry. These musing on culture and the sub- culture of African American Manhood in these critical times is a critically important message.

My Life, on the last pew his marries popular values and builds on beliefs to negative stereotypes so that in whatever form you experience it confronts cognitive mind blocks and invites a new context of thinking.

The poems contained in the volumes of “My life on the last pew” are filled with messages of confidence, crisis, despair and hope in the voice and written hand of a young black man, educated in America and by America . It is the sincere and eternal wish of the author that these words find and support someone on their journey but most itinually the young students of color in the very real classroom of America.

About Me

For over 18 years I have been an advocate of institutional equity and Social-Emotional Learning. Over the last decade, I have been extremely fortunate in collecting invaluable experiences in the classroom and communities throughout the United States. Much of my direct experience has been gained working in schools often referred to as “alternative” or “innovative learning environments”.

In the scope of my professional experience, I have served as a Residential Hall Director, Judicial Conduct Officer, Intervention Specialist, Contained classroom teacher, educational consultant, and Director. I have coordinated with many K-12 school districts on youth enrichment programming for remediation and prevention and implementing restorative discipline practices while also working with regional and national youth advocacy organizations and institutions of Higher Learning.

My own transformational educational experience was in the eleventh grade when my mother and father made a decision that would forever change the direction of my life. After watching a special on sixty minutes about a small African-American faith-based boarding school in Piney woods Mississippi, my parents would uproot me from all that was familiar and plant the seed of the Piney Woods Country Life School in Jackson, Mississippi deep in my heart.

Up until that point, I had been traveling, along with the rest of my peers, through the public -school system, receiving an education in complacency. I was successful in the 8th grade at taking all of the standardized tests required of a high school graduate yet by the time I was in 10th grade I was failing multiple classes. I was dangerously wandering the track of graduation or incarceration that seduces so many young black men. I was a happy follower, rarely exercised my ability to think freely, and I was satisfied to not stand out muffling my individuality as a youth and student. That decision to send me to Piney woods would change the direction of my life.

The mission of the school that promised to educate the hands, hearts and minds of all its students became a personal call to arms. It is why I always desire to be an active participant even if it is to just listen actively. My work speaks from my own experience and seeks first to understand, then to be understood. In whatever role we should meet I hope to participate to the fullest of my ability always remembering the goal is not always to agree but it is to gain a deeper understanding. Thank you for your inquiry and I look forward to speaking with you more in the future.
